6.1. Collecting Information from Reporter

In this section you will learn to:
  • Gather sufficient information from the reporter
One of the greatest challenges to troubleshooting any problem is gathering sufficient information from the reporter.
A standard question to ask a reporter is "What have you changed recently?" The challenge is that the reflex reply from the reporter is "Nothing." There are several reasons for this:
  1. The question is oftentimes asked in an accusatory fashion. When society is focused on placing blame for the problem on some one or some thing, we need to figure out how to put the reporter at ease. Try to gain the trust of the reporter. Focus on their observation skills, by perhaps phrasing it as: "Have you noticed any recent changes to your system/environment?"
  2. Remember that the reporter does not have our experience. They are less likely to notice significant clues or warning signs. In their mind those clues, or in this case changes, are "unrelated." Did they recently change brands of morning coffee, and now all the systems are running slower? Could the recent installation of a new wireless phone system in the building be causing sporadic networking issues?
  3. Use every problem report as an opportunity to teach the reporter how to gather more information. For example, how many reporters write down the exact error message text? In this discussion of changes, suggest that the reporter keep a log of changes they make to their system for easier recall.
